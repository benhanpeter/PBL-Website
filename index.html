Bringing rail transit
to the waterfront

A consortium of Pop Up Metro, the Delaware River Waterfront Corporation, Carnegie Mellon University, and others, are partnering to bring new passenger rail service to the waterfront, from Race St. to Dockside.

About

Pop-Up Metro (PUM) with support from the Philadelphia Belt Line (PBL) are proposing a transit demonstration project on the Southend of PBLR’s Trackage as a part of a $1 Million National Science Foundation Grant program called the Civic Innovation Program. Specifically, PUM and PBLR are interested in demonstrating battery-electric transit service between Race St. and Queen St along the Delaware River Waterfront, a 1.01-mile segment. This demonstration would represent the first battery-electric transit system in the United States and revitalize a line that has been dormant for nearly 30 years.

History

The Philadelphia Belt Line has been in freight operations since the late 1880s and is a staple of the Philadelphia Rail industry. Since inception, the railroad has maintained its policy of allowing all railroads to access the Delaware River Ports. Over 10,000 carloads cross the PBLR North trackage into Tioga Marine Terminal.

Proposed Service

PUM is formally proposing a demonstration along the PBLR South from the end of the line on Race St. to Pier 70 Blvd, a distance of roughly 1.01 miles. PUM has identified sites for six platforms along the line:
  • Race St.
  • Penn’s Landing North
  • Penn’s Landing South
  • Dockside
This demonstration will hopefully occur during popular months for Waterfront which appears to be May to September. The service would not contain a schedule but rather shuttle back and forth constantly throughout the day. PUM expects each trip to take roughly 12 minutes, with speeds not exceeding 19 mph. Station dwell time is estimated at 30 seconds to accommodate traffic delays.

What's next?

The project team is currently working to prepare the right of way for a demo planned for Fall 2026. These activities include trimming the trees along the tracks as to not conflict with the train, construction of a light maintenance and charging facility, and track maintenance work. These activities will progress throughout Summer 2026.
